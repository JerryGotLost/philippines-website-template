Climate Change
Explanation.

Our Definition: Climate change in the present includes both global warming and its effects on Earth's weather patterns. Although climate change has occurred previously, the current changes are much more rapid and not caused by natural factors. Climate change is instead caused by the emissions of greenhouse gases, these are mainly carbon dioxide and methane.
Oxford Definition: a change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.


What are Greenhouse Gases?


Human activity has been the main driver of climate change ever since the 1800s, mainly because of the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. By burning these, humans have Carbon Dioxide (CO2) rise into the atmostphere. Carbon Dioxide is a type of "Greenhouse gas" which vibrate once hit by heat energy. By vibrating, they can essentially "trap" the heat into our atmostphere. Greenhouse gas emissions, such as carbon dioxide and methane, contribute to climate change. The emissions are produced by using gasoline to drive a car or coal to heat a building, for instance. Deforestation can also create carbon dioxide emissions. In addition to garbage landfills, methane emissions are a major contributor to global warming. Energy, industry, transportation, buildings, agriculture, and land use are among the main sources.

"Human-caused global climate change is primarily due to the release, through our activities, of greenhouse gases."
